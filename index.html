How to Hire a Songwriter

Finding the right songwriter for your project starts with knowing your genre, budget, and what you want delivered — a hook, a full song, or a co-write. Browse by genre above and read each profile carefully. Most listed songwriters offer a brief consultation before committing to a project.

What Does a Songwriter Cost?

  • Entry-level / indie: $100–$300 per song
  • Mid-level / experienced: $300–$800 per song
  • Commercial / sync: $500–$2,000+ per project
  • Major label / placement deals: Negotiated per project

What is Ghostwriting in Music?

Ghostwriting means a songwriter writes lyrics or songs for an artist who releases the work under their own name. This is completely legal and common across all genres. Many artists work with ghostwriters for albums, singles, and commercial projects.
